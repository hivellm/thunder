One change at a time; re-run the matrix after each
      — **FIRST FIX LANDED (`a7568ee`): poll-once dispatch fast path.** The 1.2b question was answered directly by an A/B against the `thunder-stripped` inline lane in one run (relative → robust to this host's absolute noise): spawn-per-request cost the server **+76% on point-echo d16/c4**, the single worst G5 loss. Fix: poll the dispatch future once on the read loop; a handler that resolves without suspending (cache hit / in-mem read / the benchmark no-op) is answered inline with no task, while one that suspends on real I/O returns Pending and is still moved to a task (SRV-004 preserved, no head-of-line blocking). Synchronous panic caught + isolated like the runtime isolates a task panic (SRV-005). Invariants held (SRV-003 permit still taken, SRV-007 one serialization, CLT-011 single writer); full thunder-rpc suite green (61+ tests). **Matrix moved 3/11 → 6 WINs + 2 near-ties of 11** (High-perf, pinned, A/B, `--allow-noisy` diagnostic — NOT a citable verdict, BEN-031). Turned d16/c4 (−5%→+65%) and pipelined d1000/c4 (tie→+68%).
      — **REMAINING CELLS DECOMPOSED (diagnostic, allow-noisy, both 4- and 8-core pins).** The still-not-clearing cells were all **depth-1 + multiple connections** (synchronous round-trip, no pipeline to amortize). Two distinct causes, neither a spawn/wire defect:
        1. **Core starvation, not transport (a measurement artifact).** The bench pins 4 cores by default; Thunder's client is 3 tasks/connection (worker + reader + writer, the multiplexed-demux + coalescing design) vs the peer drivers' ~1, so at 4 conns Thunder runs 12 tasks on 4 cores — oversubscription the leaner peers dodge. Re-pinning **8 cores** (realistic server headroom) flipped **medium-4KiB d1/c4 from −10.7% to +0.3%/+17%** and lifted **pipelined d1000/c4 to +329%**. So those were not Thunder losing the transport; they were the matrix starving its task model. BEN-001 ("isolate the transport") is arguably better served by headroom ≥ the peers' task count.
        2. **Client per-call overhead in the sync-tiny regime (a real design trade-off).** `point-echo-64B d1/c4` stays at parity/slightly behind resp3 even at 8 cores (−0.2%…−18% across runs). At 64 B synchronous, the multiplexed client's per-call machinery (oneshot channel + pending-map insert/remove + the writer-task hop from `1bdbcb6` + two wakes) costs more than a trivial write-read client — the exact overhead that *amortizes to +362% under pipelining* (d1000/c1) and +66-82% at depth. Erasing it means abandoning multiplexing on the hot path, which would forfeit the pipelined wins Thunder exists for. **That is a SPEC decision (SRV/CLT), not a silent optimization (per 1.5) — raised, not taken.**
        3. **The marginal cells (medium d16) swing ±15% between runs on this host** — dispersion exceeds the ±10% question, so they are simply **not judgeable here** (BEN-031); a quiet host is required to settle them either way.
      **Net:** poll-once was the high-leverage fix and it decisively moved the weight-bearing regimes; what remains is one design-trade-off parity cell and a set of marginals lost in this machine's noise.
      — **SECOND FIX ATTEMPTED THEN REVERTED (client inline-write): hypothesis refuted, not a failure.** To close `point-echo-64B d1/c4` the leading suspect was the client's writer-task hop (`1bdbcb6`: mpsc→writer→socket per call). Built the alternative — inline write under a `TokioMutex<BufWriter<Box<dyn AsyncWrite>>>` with a **waiter-count coalescing flush** (cancel-safe via a `WaiterGuard`) so CLT-011 and the pipelined single-syscall coalescing both hold; full suite stayed green (concurrency out-of-order, reconnect, poison all pass). A/B (High-perf, 8 cores): **pipeline stayed a ~4× rout** (thunder 513k–845k vs peers 106k–203k) and point-echo d16/c4 rose to +149% — but **d1/c4 did NOT close** (thunder 57.1k vs resp3 61.4k, −7%). So the writer hop was **not** the depth-1 bottleneck: it is the **reader/oneshot demux** (a read task must receive out-of-order responses while callers await), which is **intrinsic to multiplexing** — the very thing that buys the +362% pipelined win. Reverted to `ed610a0` (conn.rs byte-identical) rather than keep a hot-path rewrite whose pipeline-absolute impact can't be validated without a quiet host and that doesn't achieve its goal. **Confirmed verdict: point-echo-64B d1/c4 is a genuine design trade-off of the multiplexed client, not a fixable defect** — closing it means dropping multiplexing, a SPEC decision (1.5), not taken.
